## GENERAL A/B TESTING NOTES

### Subject Line Anatomy (for reference)
Each subject line falls into one of these categories:

**Curiosity-Driven:**
- "We found a gap in [Company] privacy policy"
- "This one thing most [sector] miss"

**Penalty-Driven:**
- "A$5,000+ per breach from July 1"
- "What happens when the Privacy Commissioner..."

**Social Proof-Driven:**
- "247 agencies have already..."
- "Why 156 law firms are preparing now"

**Authority-Driven:**
- "We've analysed 500+ pharmacies"
- "Law Council deadline..."

**Urgency-Driven:**
- "99 days until Privacy Act deadline"
- "July 1: here's why you need to move fast"

### Testing Strategy

**Phase 1 (Week 1-2):**
- Send variant pairs: curiosity vs. penalty within same sector
- Track open rates
- Identify winner for that sector

**Phase 2 (Week 3-4):**
- Send variant pairs: social proof vs. authority within same sector
- Track open rates + click rates
- Identify winner for that sector

**Phase 3 (Week 5+):**
- Run winner from Phase 1 + winner from Phase 2 in alternating sends
- Optimise for click-through rates and conversion

**Minimum sample size:** 100 sends per variant to get statistical significance

### Tone Notes

- **Real Estate:** Practical, urgent, numbers-focused
- **Chemists:** Authoritative, patient-focused, regulation-focused
- **Lawyers:** Professional, reputation-focused, peer-driven

### Personalisation Fields

All subject lines should use [Company Name] or [First Name] where possible:
- "We found a gap in [Company Name]'s privacy policy" (higher open rate than generic)
- "Hey [First Name], quick check on your Privacy Act compliance"

### Do NOT Use

- ALL CAPS (spam trigger)
- Multiple question marks (spam trigger)
- "Urgent" or "IMMEDIATE ACTION" (overused, lower credibility)
- "Click here" or "Act now" in subject line (reduces open rate)
- Emojis (sector-specific: lawyers especially avoid)
- Numbers at the start (e.g., "3 Privacy Act gaps")—use them mid-line instead
