### Bias Risk Flags
- **Well-modelled demographics:** Sydney/Melbourne professionals (Marcus, Sarah, James, Lisa, Amita, Rashid) are likely accurately represented. Accountants and lawyers are common in training data.
- **Less-modelled demographics:** Perth/Adelaide personas (David, Frank) may be less accurately represented. Jewellers and used car dealers are less common in training data. Regional Australian small business owners have distinct communication patterns that LLMs may not capture perfectly.
- **Potential gaps:** All personas are literate, articulate to some degree. Frank's technological uncertainty is modelled, but there may be small business owners with less formal education or different communication styles not represented here. No personas from non-English-speaking backgrounds (despite Australian diversity).
- **Mitigation applied:** 
  - Assigned knowledge boundaries to prevent all personas from responding as experts
  - Voice calibration notes included to ensure variation in articulation
  - Regional personas (David, Frank, Tom) given specific behavioral patterns tied to their locations and industries
  - Attitudinal roles distributed to ensure full spectrum from enthusiast to hostile rejector

### Overall Panel Fitness: **Strong**

**Rationale:** The panel spans the target audience across sectors, geographies, tech sophistication, and attitudinal positions. All structural roles are filled. Contradictions are behavioural and specific. Knowledge boundaries are realistic and varied. The panel should reveal:
- Why some persona segments will convert (Priya, Amita - segment wants this)
- Why others won't (Elena, Frank, Tom - segment doesn't see the relevance yet)
- What messaging barriers exist (David's "why do I need this for cars"; Tanya's "does this even apply to dentists")
- Where cold email will fail (Frank, David - need relationship-based outreach)

The panel's weaknesses are known and noted: less-modelled regional demographics, and potentially insufficient representation of the least-educated segment of small business owners (though Frank and David approximate this). These should be flagged in synthesis if findings rely heavily on assumptions about this segment.
