Privacy Compliance Roadmap

General Small Business · Privacy Act 1988 · 13 Australian Privacy Principles

Getting Started: Your 6-Week Implementation Plan

This roadmap walks you through implementing the Australian Privacy Principles in your business. Each step is practical and designed for small business owners without a legal background.

Week 1 Publish your privacy policy on your website. Use the drafted policy from PrivacySorted as your starting point.
Week 2 Audit your data collection. List every place you collect personal information (forms, emails, phone, in-person).
Week 3 Set up data handling procedures. Document how staff should handle personal information day-to-day.
Week 4 Secure your data. Review passwords, access controls, and where personal information is stored.
Week 5 Prepare a breach response plan. Know what to do if personal information is compromised.
Week 6 Train your team. Brief all staff on the privacy policy and data handling procedures.

Step 1: Data Collection Audit

Before you can protect personal information, you need to know what you're collecting, where it lives, and why you have it.

Checklist

  • List all website forms that collect personal information (contact forms, newsletter signup, booking forms, checkout)
  • List all offline collection points (phone enquiries, in-person meetings, paper forms)
  • For each collection point, document: what information is collected, why it's needed, where it's stored
  • Identify any personal information you collect that you don't actually need (and stop collecting it)
  • Check if you collect any sensitive information (health, financial, government IDs) and document why
  • Review third-party services that handle your customer data (email marketing, CRM, payment processors, analytics)

Step 2: Data Handling Procedures

These are the day-to-day rules your team follows when handling personal information.

Checklist

  • Write a one-page "Data Handling Guide" for staff covering: how to store customer information, who can access it, when to delete it
  • Set up a process for customers to request access to their information (APP 12)
  • Set up a process for customers to request corrections to their information (APP 13)
  • Document how you respond to privacy complaints (who handles them, expected timeframes)
  • If you send marketing emails, ensure opt-out works and is processed within 5 business days (APP 7)
  • If you share data with overseas services (cloud storage, email tools), document which countries and why (APP 8)

Step 3: Security Measures

APP 11 requires you to take reasonable steps to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access.

Checklist

  • Use strong, unique passwords for all business accounts (use a password manager)
  • Enable two-factor authentication on email, CRM, and cloud storage accounts
  • Limit staff access to customer data on a need-to-know basis
  • Ensure your website uses HTTPS (SSL certificate)
  • Back up customer data regularly (and test that backups work)
  • Set a data retention schedule: delete personal information you no longer need
  • Secure physical records (locked cabinets, restricted access areas)
  • Review and remove access for former employees immediately on departure

Step 4: Breach Response Plan

If personal information is compromised, you may need to notify the OAIC and affected individuals under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme.

Checklist

  • Designate a breach response lead (usually the business owner for small businesses)
  • Document the steps to follow if a breach is suspected: contain, assess, notify
  • Know the threshold: you must notify if the breach is likely to result in serious harm
  • Prepare a template breach notification (to OAIC and to affected individuals)
  • Keep the OAIC notification form bookmarked: oaic.gov.au/privacy/notifiable-data-breaches
  • Test your response plan with a scenario (e.g., "laptop with customer data is stolen")

Step 5: Staff Training

Privacy compliance only works if your whole team understands their responsibilities.

Checklist

  • Brief all staff on the privacy policy (what it says, why it matters)
  • Share the Data Handling Guide with all staff who touch customer information
  • Cover the basics: don't share customer info without permission, lock screens when away, report suspected breaches immediately
  • Include privacy training in onboarding for new staff
  • Schedule a 6-monthly refresher (15 minutes is enough)

Step 6: Ongoing Compliance

Privacy compliance is not a one-time task. Set a schedule to keep things current.

Checklist

  • Review and update your privacy policy annually (or when your data practices change)
  • Re-audit your data collection points when you add new services, forms, or tools
  • Monitor the OAIC website for guidance updates relevant to your sector
  • Run a PrivacySorted scan after policy updates to verify your compliance score
  • Keep records of your compliance activities (audits, training dates, policy updates)

Key date: From 1 July 2026, the small business exemption is being removed for AML-CTF reporting entities. If you're not sure whether this applies to your business, check the OAIC guidance or consult a privacy professional.
